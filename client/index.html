    Rules

    1. You must try to explain as many words as possible in limited time.
    2. You cannot say the word or use any of the four banned words in your explanation.
    3. Rule 2 applies to compound words and derived words that use the same stem as in your word or one of the four banned words as well.
      E.g. you can't say “fire”, “truck” or “fire brigade” if your word is “fire truck”.
    4. As the explainer, you cannot speak another language if you don't know a word. Explain your way around it.
      You are, however, allowed to use a dictionary if you think that is a worthwhile use of your very limited time.
    5. As the guesser, you must say the exact word—synonyms don't count.
      However as the explainer you're allowed to say that the guesser is close and is looking for a synonym.
    6. You're only allowed to guess and explain based on the semantics of the word.
      I.e. you're not allowed to give meta information such as the amount of characters, amount of vowels etc.
    7. You cannot use proper nouns.
